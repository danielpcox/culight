Sudan
"...Africa's largest country"

Tensions in Sudan between the Arab Muslims in the north and mainly African Christian and pagan population in the south have led to two civil wars. 

In a country 1/4 the size of the U.S., over 2 million people have been murdered, killed in action, or starved due to war. The south has been devastated- 5 million people have lost their homes and half a million have fled as refugees to other

In 2003, a separate conflict began in the Darfur region of Western Sudan. At least 200,000 have been killed and nearly 2 million displaced; UN peacekeeping troops are still struggling to stabilize the situation.

Sudan was declared an Islamic Republic in 1083 and have made attempts since then to Islamize the country- introducing shari'a law and turning schools into Muslim schools.
