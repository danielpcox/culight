Missions Organizations
Below is a sampling of the missions organizations through which students from Cornell and Ithaca have previously gone on missions trips. Please contact the person listed with each organization if you have any questions or concerns about the organization, previous summer trips and experiences, etc.!
OMF International
Founded by Hudson Taylor in 1865, OMF International serves the church and brings the gospel to some 12 countries in East Asia and has a pioneering ministry in others. OMF helps place Christians to share the love of Christ with East Asians worldwide.
www.us.omf.org

Pioneers
Pioneers mobilizes teams to glorify God among unreached peoples by initiating church-planting movements in partnership with local churches. Founded nearly thirty years ago, today there are more than 1,700 international members serving on 178 teams in 80 countries among 130 unreached people groups.
www.pioneers.org

V-Set
V-SET, Volunteer Summer English Teachers, was established in 1989, with a desire to create a volunteer movement among the people with the purpose of teaching English to students. This organization is mainly designed to maximize relational and social opportunities with students. V-SET is an 8-week program where 1 week is spent in the United States, a week overseas, and 4-5 weeks of teaching English, and the last week for debriefing.
www.v-set.org
Specific Area: China
Contact: Hannah Chen '10 (hannah.f.chen@gmail.com)

eMi (Engineering Ministries International)
eMi was founded in July 1974, through a desire to blend the design professions and faith in Christ to expand God's kingdom. This organization serves the poor in developing countries. They provide with facilities such as buildings, roads, hospitals, water supplies, and electricity that directly helps the people and help show God's love. eMi partners with local churches, Church organizations, and other non profits who share the same vision to help reach out to the poor and to share the Gospel of Jesus Christ.
www.emiusa.org
Specific Area: Developing countries; specifically for the design professions
Contact: Joy Lee (wjl23@cornell.edu)

Jerusalem Ministry
Jerusalem Ministry is a non-profit Christian organization set up mainly for orphaned and abandoned children. JM help trains, equips, and holds accountable for the volunteers in the childrens homes in Seoul, Korea. Many of the volunteers are at least committed for 6 months and are mainly teachers of English, Korean, music, and art. Jerusalem Ministry has 2 main purposes: 1) "To build committed and loving relationships with the underprivileged children of Seoul through the avenue of education." 2) "To train and equip volunteers for effective service, and hold them accountable both to their commitment to the children, and to their relationship with God."
www.jerusalemministry.org
Specific Area: South Korean orphanages

Go Live Serve
Do you feel called to a profession and to overseas ministry? God may be calling you to be a Tentmaker. Merge your education, career, and ministry. Go. Live. Serve. is a sending agency committed to mobilizing, equipping and sending committed Christian students and professionals to serve and reach the unreached in the megacities of the 10/40 Window. Tentmakers are ambassadors for Christ, self-supporting like the apostle Paul, who made tents for a living. Tentmaking provides both work and ministry platforms. By doing a good job, tentmakers contribute to society and demonstrate how Christian values are lived out in the real world. By reaching out to their colleagues, they have natural connections with local people, who are part of their everyday lives. Professions can provide good entry points to places that do not welcome traditional missionaries.
www.goliveserve.org
Specific Area: East and Central Asia
