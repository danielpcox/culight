Light 10 is a night of praise, exhortation, prayer for the nations, and resources for Summer 2010 mission trips. Our hope is that Christians in Ithaca and all over the world would know God's heart for the nations and be encouraged to pray, send and go.
What is the history behind Light 10?
Briefly put, Light 10 is the fusing of two missions mobilization/information/prayer nights that were held in the past at Cornell University, which included KCCE's CUStraightAhead and the Student Volunteer Movement (SVM2). CU Light was born in 2009.
When/where is Light 10?
Light 10 will be held on January 30, 2010 in BioTech G10 from 7:00 - 9:30PM. (Here is a Google Map to the BioTech building, which is between and behind the Lynah skating rink and Teagle hall.)
 
What's the schedule for the night?
7:00-7:20   Worship
7:20-8:00   Exhortation (Alex Lee, Cornell '07)
8:00-8:10   Response/prayer and Video
8:10-9:00   Prayer for the Nations
9:00-9:30   Closing Worship and Prayer
What is "Prayer for the Nations"?
From roughly 8:00 to 9:00, we will have 12 "country stations" set up around the room with information and prayer requests for each nation, as well as pictures and videos. During the prayer time, attendees will be able to move freely around the room to intercede for the nations.
Welcome to the website of Light 10!
Based on the missions event of the same name, this website exists to continue the vision of Light 10 throughout the rest of the year by educating, encouraging, supporting, and providing resources about world missions to the body of Christ in Ithaca and beyond.
On this site you will find:
  • Information and prayer requests for nations
  • Opportunities to go on missions (summer 2010 and longterm)
  • Testimonies from Ithacans who have gone on trips in the past
  • Support information for those who will be going on missions
  • Other resources such as pictures, videos, and noteworthy quotes pertaning to missions
  • Links to various groups affiliated with Light 10
  • Registration for our email list
  • In the future, we hope to set up an online fund to support those going on mission trips from Cornell and Ithaca.
